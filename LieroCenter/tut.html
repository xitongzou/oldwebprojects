  Digging

Digging is one of, if not the most important abilities in Liero. Most of the Liero terrains are enclosed and you cannot get around without digging (unless you blow your way through with a weapon but that's another story).

To basic dig, hold the direction you would like to move (left or right) [point A] and rapidly press the opposite direction [point B] to dig the way that you are holding. Confused yet? Here's an example.

You would like to dig left. Hold left and rapidly press right. [hold < + >>>>>>>>]

Simple enough. So you can dig left and right but you still need to dig up. Simple. Aim downward and rapidly press both directions.

To dig downward, aim downward and rapidly press left and right. [<><><><><><>]

Now, the hardest part of digging is digging upwards. The reason why is because you can't. The maximum you can dig is 45 degrees that is approximately this equivalent to this: /. However there is a way to get up but it is extremely difficult. First, dig upward, the allotted 45 degrees, then jump up the opposite direction and dig upward. You should have created a small hole that you will be able to just climb into and continue digging that way. Continue this pattern and you should be able to get to your high destination.

 

  Roping

Roping is very essential tool in a Liero terrain where it is very open and also when you need to reach high parts that are virtually impossible to reach otherwise. To initiate the rope, hold the weapon select button and then press jump.

What you are supposed to do is aim where you would like to attach your rope to, then hold the weapon select button and press jump. If the terrain you are aiming for is out of the rope's length and reach, the rope will automatically fall until it finds a nearby piece of land to attach itself to. If it finds no land, it will automatically attach itself to the ground.

While hanging on the rope, you have many options. You can hang up their and drop weapons, continuously rope around as transportation or to avoid being hit by your enemy or use it to reach an out of reach destination. If you are trying to use it as transportation or to avoid your enemy's weapons, aim to where you want to shoot it, hold the weapon select button, then press jump. Now you are hanging in the air on the rope. Now, re-aim where you want to shoot the next shot and press the jump button. Now, re aim where you want to the next shot and press the jump button, etc.

Now that you know how to multiple rope around the terrain, you need to reach a part of the terrain which is virtually impossible to reach any other way. Aim near the approximate area of where it is. Now once you have it in sight, aim at the ceiling right above where you want to go or aim at the ground of your destination. Press the jump button. Once you've reached your destination, you can go ahead and let go of the weapon select button to end your roping session.

I bet you think you know everything there is to know about roping, right? Wrong. Although there is an entire world of roping styles, objectives and play, these are the absolute basics and necessities of roping. To end the tutorial, you will learn how to drop weapons onto unsuspecting victims below, behind or above. If your enemy is somewhere below you, chose a weapon a nuke like weapon (Mini Nuke, Big Nuke, etc.) or a Spikeball type weapon. Now aim downward and press fire. Hopefully, if your aim was correct, your enemy has been injured. Next, to aim at your enemy who is on the terrain near you or roping beside you. You may want to choose a Flamer if he's very close to you or a Minigun type weapon if he's further. You can aim your weapon in his general direction and press fire and he'll be blown to bits. Finally, if your enemy is above you, you can either choose a Minigun type weapon or a Nuke type weapon. Aim upward and fire your weapon at him. Hopefully he'll be falling to his bloody death.

Other helpful roping uses are attaching yourself to your enemy who's on a rope where you aim at your enemy's body and try and attach yourself to him. Wherever he goes, you will follow unless you drop your connection to the rope. You can also fire a weapon to break the terrain that your enemy's rope is attached to sending him and his rope plummeting to the ground. There are many uses for the rope, see if you can find your own.
