MAJIN CELL

Or

The Super Potara Fusion-Waza

By Crystal Gryphon

 

                Silence.  There was always that horrible, oppressive, silence. If there was one thing to be held above another about this place, it was the silence.  It was not as he’d imagined such a place to be—not that he’d dedicated much or any time imagining any particular place in the afterlife.  But somehow, he’d envisioned suffering to be more…vocal.

                Yet, souls here kept their misery to themselves in an annoying, noble sort of way.

                Nearby, a portal opened and a messenger stepped through.  Some nameless ogre on some pointless mission, carrying it out with unappreciated dedication.  The fellow hurried off to find the Task Master of this level as if his errand were all-important.  How important could anything be once you were dead?  For that matter, what was the point in hurrying anywhere once you were here?

                Cell watched with a curiosity born of boredom.  The portal remained opened even though the ogre had left the area in search of his superior on this level.  Careless of him.  Cell smiled.  Seems the all-powerful King Enma did not screen his workers for being meticulous.

                And still, the portal was open.  A beckoning invitation.  Who was he to ignore such an invitation?  Cell considered his actions.  What result would come of stepping through the portal, who could say?  But it would be better than having to listen to that conceited, boring loser, Furiiza, rant on about his race superiority one more time.  Cell took a step.  And then another.  Why not?  There was nothing as unobserved as the obvious.  Cell strolled casually through the portal.

                And stared.  This was obviously a higher level. The landscape was lighter, less oppressive.  Various inhabitants of this level were busily engaged tending to assorted tasks.

                “You there!” 

                Cell turned, affecting all innocence, facing the ogre who had singled him out.  “Me?”

                “Yes, you. Why aren’t you working?”  The ogre approached him, task sheet in hand, scanning it.

                “I’m new.  No one has told me what my job is yet,” Cell replied glibly, smiling.

                “Ah, good.  In that case you can get to work sorting all those extra malevolent energy plasma pods King Enma keeps sending down.  Over here. I’ll show you.”

                Cell considered the offer and decided it might be worth his while to continue this charade for a little longer. Who could tell what might come of it?  At the worst, it only involved a little work and might even prove an entertaining distraction.  Especially if he got to see the irresponsible ogres taken to task and punished for their stupidity.  A criminal of his caliber should not have been allowed to escape the heavily guarded bottom level.

                Cell had only started lifting over the pods when a portal opened.  An ogre with a very important air about him came storming out.

                “Ezzo don’t you have those pods moved yet?  Enma has another dozen ready to send down!”

                “Does it look to you like I have plenty of extra help around here?” the first ogre retorted sarcastically, gesturing.

                Cell stepped away from the impending argument, unnoticed.  This demon had also left his portal open and Cell smiled at such a streak of unlikely luck. Enma’s ogres must be practicing some sort of open-door policy today.  He walked through the portal.

                And halted in surprise.  This portal unexpectedly led straight to the processing station of King Enma.  A press of newcomers was jostling in line and harried ogres were rushing about.  King Enma himself was focused on a loud conversation with a very short old woman dressed in black.  Short? Squat would be a better word.  She and Enma were obviously in the middle of a heated topic and Cell’s abrupt arrival went unnoticed.

                “…treat me like I am some shuttle for running your errands. I do have a life.  You are just taking advantage of an old woman.”

                “But Baba, you are the perfect person to run back and forth between the two realms.  Who can so easily cross the portals,” Enma insisted, towering over the tiny woman.

                “Flattery will get you no where, King Enma,” Baba replied, hands on hips, standing up to Enma’s imposing bulk.

                Cross realms?  This odd little black toad woman had portal capability between here and the  land of the living?  Access to the living?  Cell latched onto that thought in an instant.  There was no time to debate a course of action.  Just as swiftly, he crossed the room and snatched the woman up, at the same time extending his tail.  He absorbed her before Enma could react, could think of reacting. Knowledge was his.  Cell processed swiftly what he now knew.  Travel between the two realms was his to command.

                Enma was on his feet with a roar of outrage.  “Release her at once!”  Ogres trembled and cowered under their master’s obvious fury.

                Cell took a step back and smiled.  “I don’t think so.  Restore my life.”  After all, what good would travel to the other world do him if he were still dead?

                “Not a chance, Cell.  Now let Baba go!”  Enma thundered.

                Cell’s smile widened.  “I can make certain she never leaves my body and you know that.  Restore my life.”

                Enma stiffened.  Cell was not bluffing and as long as Baba was inside Cell, he could do nothing against Cell for fear of harming Baba. But it went against all scruples to bargain with the likes of such scum as Cell…

                Enma brought his fists down on his desk, cracking it.  “Goku and the other Saiya-jin stopped you before.  They will stop you again.  This is pointless, Cell.”  He would try reasoning, dampen Cell’s scheming with hard, cold facts.

                “My life…” Cell insisted.

                Enma drew a short breath.  Goku and the other Saiya-jin had stopped Cell once.  It looked as if they would have to do it again.

                Enma lifted his hand and Cell felt a sudden rush run hotly through his body as life coursed in his veins.  He could feel.  FEEL.  Cell wasted no further time exulting in his victory.  He conjured a portal and disappeared.

                “I’ve got to warn Goku…” Enma muttered unhappily to himself.  Then anger fused his face and he bellowed to his company of ogres.  “WHO IS RESPONSIBLE FOR LEAVING PORTALS OPEN!!”  Now it would not be hell to pay, but earth, for such a mistake.

                Cell paused to draw a deep breath.  He did not require air to live, but feeling it fill his lungs, move across his face, was invigorating.  To feel again… Never had he felt such a keen sense of aliveness.  Or revenge.  The Saiya-jin would pay for what they had done.  They were here.  All of them. He could sense their ki. Cell frowned.  And something else was here. Something incredibly powerful that was not Saiya-jin.  It was beyond any power he’d every felt.

                Cell stirred mesmerized by such a power.  He must have it!  Cell launched skyward, tracking this incredible ki to the source.  Below was a park where humans were happily at play in blissful ignorance.  Cell strained to locate the exact source of that ki.  And stared.  An enormous pink blob was sitting on a bench alone, eating something.  Cell’s jaw dropped.  That energy came from this creature?  He shook off his shock and landed lightly beside the pink blob.

                Majin Buu looked up and grinned happily.  “Hi.  Want to play?”

                Cell gawked, unable for a moment to contend with the fact that such incredible power was governed by such a feeble mind.  He smiled very slowly.

                “Yessss.  Let’s…play.” 

Bulma ran her hand across her stomach, feeling the ripple of another contraction.  She grimaced and Chi-Chi noticed her expression.

                “Another contraction, Bulma?” Chi Chi inquired solicitously. She set aside the stack of baby clothing.  She and Videl had come over today to help Bulma with setting up the nursery.

                “Oh, it’s nothing,” Bulma replied, sitting down.

                “Nothing my foot,” Videl exclaimed, excited.  “You could have that baby any day now, Bulma.” 

                “Dat?  Dat?”  Videl scooped up her own little toddler, Pan, now one year.  The baby wriggled free and tottered away to play with a toy.

                “Is it my imagination or is Vegita hanging around the house more these days?” Chi-Chi asked, glancing out the window to the balcony where her husband and the Saiya-jin prince were conversing. 

                Bulma smiled, happy Chi Chi had noticed.  While Vegita had not said anything to her, she could tell he’d taken an interest of sorts as she’d neared the end of her pregnancy.  Whether he was pleased or not about having a daughter, she wasn’t sure—but he’d certainly been around more lately. 

                “Trunks gotten over his disappointment he isn’t having a brother?” Chi-Chi asked.

                “Yeah.  Goten is more a brother to him to make up for it,” Bulma replied.  “I can’t believe the boys are so grown up.”  Trunks had just turned thirteen and Goten was twelve.  The thought struck her that she was getting old herself!

Outside on the balcony, Goku frowned as the debate he and Vegita had been having on the creation of a genki dama while is SSJ mode was interrupted.  His attention shifted elsewhere a moment and Vegita waited. Goku looked up, frowning.

                “Enma needs to see us.”  Goku did not wait to see if Vegita would be opposed to coming along, but clapped his hand on the Prince’s shoulder and pressed two fingers to his forehead and teleported. 

                Vegita slapped Goku’s hand away as they appeared before King Enma in his processing station.  Everything was in a state of uproar.

                “What is wrong, Pops?” Goku asked, glancing curiously at the energetic ogres dashing about.

                “Goku, Vegita.  I am glad to see  you .  I have a problem,” Enma began, his brows furrowed in worry.

                “How is that our problem?” Vegita countered, implying Enma’s problems were his alone. 

                “Cell has escaped.  I won’t bore you with the details on how.”

                “So send your ogres to hunt him down,” Goku replied, not seeing how that constituted an emergency. This realm was under the rule of Enma and tracking escaped souls was probably rather commonplace.

                “Escaped to where?” Vegita asked, at once suspicious.  The Demon King would not have summoned their help for tracking escapees in this realm, and the Prince had a sudden premonition prickle the hair on the back of his neck.

                “To earth,” Enma confessed, looking even more miserable.  “He escaped the lower levels and then absorbed Baba.  I am sorry to tell you this.”

                Goku turned to Vegita and stared in shock.

                “You let Cell…escape.”  Vegita folded his arms, his condemnation unspoken.

                “Oh great,” Goku said when he could think of nothing else to say.

                “I didn’t exactly….let him…” Enma muttered, twisting his hands together.  “You have to find him and free Baba.”

                “Free—You mean Cell has absorbed someone?” Vegita demanded.

                “Baba…Roshi’s sister?” Goku exclaimed.

                Vegita stared at Enma.  “If this story is going to get any worse, you had better tell us right now.”  He was familiar with the powers Baba possessed.  That explained how Cell had traveled to the realm of the living again.  Partly—anyway.

                “Cell came here and absorbed Baba.  I had to grant him life or he would have destroyed Baba.”

                Goku was struggling to follow the scenario.  “Wait a minute.  Cell escaped.  He got to the station here and absorbed Baba and you gave him life again.”

                Vegita raised an eyebrow in impatience while Goku sorted through the situation.

                “That’s not good,” Goku decided.  “It took all we had to stop Cell before.  Imagine if he were to absorb—“

                Vegita had only been half-listening to Goku and his head snapped around in sudden realization of what Goku had just implied.  Goku met his gaze.

                “Buu!” they said at the same time.

                “I told you, you would regret not letting me destroy that fat, pink blob,” Vegita growled.  Both of them focused their thoughts back on earth. 

                “Too late,” Goku stated in dismay.  Buu’s ki had disappeared.

                Goku wasted no time in teleporting them back to earth.  They hovered mid-air, searching. 

                “It’s no use.  I can’t sense Buu and Cell is suppressing his ki.  This isn’t good.  It took all we had to beat Buu last time,” Goku said.  That had been six years ago. And now Cell had that unlimited power of Buu at his beck.  They would be in for the fight of their lives.

                “Fusion is our only chance,” Goku after a moment of silence.

                Vegita rolled his eyes, annoyed.  “Why do you always have to bring that up?”

                “Gojita would stand a chance and you know it,” Goku ventured.  “We don’t.”

                The Prince gave that serious consideration.  “I am not sure.  Even with Gojita’s power it might not be enough. And the thirty minute time constraint may be too short.”  Thirty minutes for beating a Buu-enhanced Cell left a lot to be desired.  Like time, for one thing.  Gojita could locate Cell in seconds and they could use Goku’s Shunkan-Idou technique to teleport there at once, but would Gojita have enough power?

                “If Gohan performed Fusion-Waza with Gojita, we could up our power,” Goku suggested.

                “Not possible.”  Vegita shook his head, dismissing the idea.  “Gohan’s power range is very high, but Gogeta would outclass him to the point it would not be possible to equalize to perform fusion.”

                Goku considered that.  “But not Gotenks.”

                Vegita met Goku’s eyes, aware that for once, Goku had come up with something useful.  If anyone on the planet could come close to matching Gojita’s power level, it would be Gotenks.

                “Can a multiple fusion be performed?” Vegita asked, ever practical.  To date, only two people had successfully completed a fusion.  This would be four people and was untried.  It might not even be possible.

                “I know someone we can ask,” Goku replied.  “King Kai, are you following this?”  Goku winged his thought to his mentor and friend. 

                Yes, I am Goku. Sorry to hear you are in so much trouble.  Yes, a multiple fusion is possible, but your time would be reduced owing to the strain of holding a multiple fusion.  Kai had indeed been following events and responded at once.  He fully intended to have a word with Enma over such a foolish mistake placing the earth in such grave danger.

                Well, they had an answer. 

                “So we can up the strength, but lose some time,” Goku summed up.  A trade-off of time for strength.  At least it was possible.  A blast of powerful ki erupted on the planet below.

                “We’d better call everyone together and tell them the bad news,” Goku said grimly.  He telepathically aimed a call at the other Z fighters, instructing them to meet at Dende’s in order to brief them on the situation.

The Z-Senshi stirred, the shock of what was confronting them, silencing any comments. Even the wind seemed mournful as it blew across the white tiles, rustling the leaves of the few trees. Goku wisely let the bad news sink in.

                “So not only do we have to defeat Cell again, we have to free Baba and Buu,” Krillin recapped after a few moments.

                “And you propose fusion yourself and Vegita to face Cell as Gojita,” Gohan guessed. 

                “Well, not exactly.  We thought we’d have Gojita do fusion with Gotenks,” Goku explained.

                Goten and Trunks stared at each other then whooped in joy.  Goku had to shush their exuberant response.  Piccolo stared, startled.

                “Will that work, Goku?” Piccolo asked, skeptical.

                “Kai says it will, but we won’t have as much time.”

                “It will never happen,” Piccolo declared.  “Gotenks and Gojita will be too stuck-up and conceited to consider working together.” If there was one thing he’d learned from Saiya-jin fusion, the resulting person had twice the ego of Vegita.

                “The rest of you need to be ready to distract Cell when the fusion wears off.  And keep Cell from absorbing anyone else,” Goku instructed.

                “Yeah, we don’t need Cell getting any more powerful by sucking up one of you guys,” Krillin noted unhappily.

                “You can bring the injured to me,” Dende offered.  “My power for healing may be spread thin, but I will do what I can.”

                “When are we gonna fight Cell?” Goten demanded.

                “Yeah. I want to kick some Cell butt!” Trunks exclaimed.  Vegita smiled tolerantly at his exuberance. 

                “Actually, how about right now?” Goku said.

                Vegita clicked his tongue in annoyance, not caring to do that stupid dance in front of everyone.

                “This I got to see,” Krillin whispered to Tien.  He’d witnessed the boys doing fusion, and he’d seen Gojita, but he’d never actually seen Goku and Vegita do the fusion together.  Vegita scowled and took up the position even as the boys popped up and seemed to explode into Gotenks.  The resulting energy nearly blew everyone off heaven.

                The short but mighty warrior sneered his contempt for his enemy.  “You don’t need Gogita. I can fight Cell by myself!” he declared.

                “Oh, really.”

                Gotenks turned around, startled by that smooth challenge to his statement of superiority.  Gojita stood there.  Smiling.

                “Wow,” Krillin muttered, awed by the ki he was reading.  Tien and Yamcha could only stare.  Gohan smiled and tried to set aside his worry.

                “Feh!” Piccolo exclaimed.

                “Do fusion with me, little warrior,” Gojita ordered.  Defiant, Gotenks started to fly off, but his mad dash was rudely interrupted.  Gojita caught him by the ankle and slammed him down several levels below the tiles.  The impact shook everyone off his or her feet. A few moments later an indignant Gotenks climbed out of the hole.

                “Hey!”

                “Fusion,” Gojita repeated firmly, taking up the pose.

                Piccolo laughed.  “Finally someone who can manage Gotenks’ ego!”

                The mighty midget warrior acquiesced and completed fusion with Gojita.  This time everyone was blown clear off heavens and the trees were snapped off at their base.  Popo and Dende hung suspended, staring in disbelief with the others.

                “Look at that!  Look at  him!”  Krillin exclaimed.

                “Who…who are you?” Yamcha dared ask.

                The figure below them did not glance up, but put two fingers to his forehead and disappeared.

                “Come on, everybody!” Krillin ordered. 

                Cell looked up, startled, at first not sure what to make of the figure who had just teleported in front of him.  There was a familiarity about the man—and not just in the gold hair and green eyes.

                “Who are you?” Cell demanded, curious.  He could sense multiple ki.  A form of absorption with the Saiya-jin?  “How interesting.  And here I thought I would have to settle for some old-fashioned, boring revenge.” Somehow the Saiya-jin had created a fused super-being.

                “I am Gojitenks and you will answer to me.”

                “Really?” Cell replied easily.  “I don’t think so.”

                Gojitenks collided with Cell, delivering a titanic blow to his face.  Cell fell back, stunned, surprised. 

                “Well, you’ve raised your power, I give you that much.”  He responded with a brutal series of punches.  Their exchange began in earnest, each side inflicting damage.  Cell broke off and used Buu’s power to pop out some serious body injury.

                “It does not matter how strong you are, you cannot defeat me,” Cell declared. His original amusement was fading.  This fused being was able to deal out some serious punishment and that hurt.

                They collided again in a vicious exchange.  Cell was landing as many punches as Gojitenks. Gojitenks broke off and leveled a ki attack at Cell.  Cell avoided the strike and sent off his own volley in rapid fire. Gojitenks took the hit full on.  Cell laughed, but was immediately dismayed to see the damage from his attack was limited.  Gojitenks dove low and grabbed Cell, throwing him upward.  Gojitenks immediately followed up with a Final Flash before Cell could recover from tumbling skyward.  Smoke cleared to reveal Cell had lost both legs.  He looked down, appearing to be a bit surprised.  Cell focused his power and regenerated new limbs.  With a howl of rage he slammed into Gojitenks.

                “Time is up,” Gohan said anxiously, aware fusion would end very soon.  Cell was taking some  heavy battle damage, but he simply kept drawing on the regenerative powers of Cell.  Four-way fusion produced a very powerful being, but there simply wasn’t enough time.

                Cell fell back as fusion was dissolved.

                “Now,” Krillin yelled.  Everyone swept in, grabbing one of the Saiya-jin and retreated back to heaven.  Gohan carefully put his dad down.  Krillin had Trunks and Piccolo held Vegita. Goten bent over, groaning as Tien set him down. 

                “How come we didn’t win,” Trunks moaned.  Dende went to the boys first, using his power as he knelt over Goten. There were serious injuries.  Broken ribs, a fractured thigh and a lacerated kidney along with numerous cuts and bruises. 

                “Their injuries are identical,” Krillin noticed, looking at the weary group as Dende worked through them.

                “How come we didn’t hurt Cell?” Trunks said.  He rotated his arm carefully, delighted to find it no longer hurt.  Dende knelt over Goku, extending his hands.

                “We did hurt him, Trunks,” Goku replied.  “But with Buu’s energy, he can continually renew himself.”  Dende pushed back from bending over the Saiya-jin Prince, spent from his efforts.

                “I…can’t heal anyone else for  24 hours,” Dende warned, exhausted.  Gohan caught his friend when Dende stumbled and would have fallen.  Healing four at once had drained him of energy.

                “Thank you, Dende,” Goku said graciously, mindful of the young Namek’s sacrifice and not wanting Dende to feel badly about being of further help.

                “We were really strong and Cell still pounded us,” Goten lamented.  Defeat was something that did not settle well with both young Saiya-jin.

                Vegita sat up and something caught his attention.

                “Company,” he warned, locking in on a approaching ki.

                On the heels of his statement, Kaioushin teleported to their  location.

                “I came as quickly as I could,”  Kaioushin said anxiously.  “I have been watching what is happening.  I saw you perform  Fusion-Waza  and I went first to talk to Rou Kaioushin for ideas.  He agrees with me that you have only one option.”

                Kaioushin reached up and pulled off his Potara earrings, holding them out to Goku. The Saiya-jin accepted them, frowning.

                “Potara fusion?”

                Kaioushin shrugged. “It gets you around the time limitations…”

                “Don’t look at me,” Vegita scowled.

                “Vegitto is stronger than Gojita to begin with,” Kaioushin said, appealing to the Saiya-jin Prince. “Start with him and do Fusion-Waza with Gotenks.  That will leave you thirty minutes.”

                “Permanent fusion?” Gohan said, not liking the idea as he glanced at the two boys.

                “No.  Permanent only for Vegitto,” Kaioushin corrected. “Vegitto’s power levels  starts out at nearly the level of Gotenks fused with Gojita.  Then he can add Gotenks through Fusion-Waza.”

                Gohan said nothing.  He did not care to think of his father permanently fused, but knowing the boys would not be included in permanent fusion helped a little.  Goku too, glanced at the boys, probably thinking the same thing.  

                “It might work,” Vegita mused.

                “Vegitto is that strong?” Gohan asked, dubious.

                “You have not met Vegitto,” Goku laughed.

                “I have,” Dende said quietly.  “His power truly scares me.”

                Goku put on an earring and tossed the other to Vegita.

                “Father, are you sure about this?” Gohan hedged.  Goku and Vegita would pay the ultimate price for this plan to work.

                “Sure,” Goku replied easily. As long he knew the two boys would be free of the fusion and not affected by the Potara, he had no qualms being fused for life with Vegita.

                The Saiya-jin Prince glared at the earring and at Goku.  Goku grinned but did not press him.  He knew Vegita well enough by now to know he would grumble, but he would do what was necessary.

                “Gojita was bad enough, but this again…”

                “Daddy wait.  You mean those earrings make fusion permanent?” Goten asked.

                “Pappa?” Trunks began, uncertain.  Both boys looked at each other, considering the implications for their fathers.

                Trunks shrugged.  “Might be cool.  We’d have the same dad.  It would be like us being Gotenks all the time.” 

                Goten did not look quite as certain but he did not object.

                Vegita put on the earring.

                The flash of light caused everyone to shield his or her eyes.  When they could all see again, Vegitto was standing there. 

                “Whoa,” Krillin said in a very small voice, awed by the ki he was reading.

                “His ki must be nearly equal to Cell right now,” Piccolo said, amazed.

                Vegitto turned to look at him.  “I am evenly matched in ki with Cell.”

                “So the fusion with Gotenks will give you the edge on power,” Gohan surmised.  He walked up very close to Vegitto.  He could not sense hi father’s ki or Vegita’s ki.  Yet the ki of this person was uniquely familiar.

                “Those two need to sleep right now,” Vegitto said, pointing at the boys.  The Namek guardian could heal the body but not restore expended energy.

                “Your energy must also be low,” Piccolo said as Vegitto turned, obviously intending to leave.  Vegitto did not answer but placed two fingers to his forehead and teleported.

                “Whoa.  I thought Gojita was powerful,” Krillin breathed.

                “Man, what power,” Yamcha agreed.  “You’re right Dende.  That guy is scary.”

                “Both of them together like that.”  Tien shook his head.  “I hope the guy doesn’t have Vegita’s ego.”

                “Someone with that much power doesn’t need an ego,” Krillin observed and shivered.  “Sure is weird.  For a moment you sort of see Goku in an expression or gesture, and then he says or does something that is more like Vegita…”

                “Come on you two. You heard…Vegitto.  You need to sleep and rest before the next fight,” Gohan said. “Let’s get to Capsule Corporation and find some empty beds.”  The boys looked like they wanted to object, but it was obvious they were spent from the first fight.

                “I wish I could be of more help,” Kaioushin said softly.  “I can teleport everyone and save energy that way.”

                “Really?” Yamcha said.

                Gohan smiled at his friend.  “Thanks Kaioushin. That will be a big help--especially when the next fight starts and we have to locate Cell quickly.”

                “I wonder where Vegitto went,” Krillin asked of no one in particular.

 

 

                Cell stood, carefully checking himself.  Everything seemed to be repaired.  The level of regeneration he had from that pink blob was impressive.  What the Saiya-jin had managed to concoct with their fusion games had taken him by surprise and cost him dearly.  It did not matter.  They would be back.  And they would pay for the pain they had caused.  He turned around and nearly jumped out of his skin.  A stranger stood there.

                “Wha—!” Cell drew back, startled.  At first impression he would have said he was facing Goku.  Or the Saiya-jin Prince.  Neither and both.  Another fusion?

                “I am Vegitto. I am the instrument of your death.”

                Cell was more curious that alarmed.  “Vegitto?  But there are just two of you in this form.”  And that did not make sense because he could read far more power from this individual than he’d sensed from that combined fusion form he’d faced only a little while earlier.

                “I will return to fight you, Cell.  Wait for me and do not trouble the earth people.”

                “Wait for you?  Why?  What do you intend to do to me?” Cell rejoined with confidence.  They had not hurt him beyond his ability to recover in the first confrontation.  What made them think they could do otherwise in a second confrontation?

                Vegitto smiled.  “I will dissect you into pieces so small that you will not be able to use Buu’s regeneration to put yourself back together.”

                “You are all big talk,” Cell replied easily, untroubled.  “Why don’t you fight me now?” 

                “In a few hours,” Vegitto said.  He lifted two fingers to his forehead and teleported.

                Cell stared.  There was a memory that belonged to the pink blob floating around in the back of his mind.  It was a very unpleasant one.  About Vegitto.  Cell decided this Vegitto might be trouble after all.

               

               

                “I don’t understand any of this,” Chi Chi lamented coming out of the room.  Both boys were asleep.  Videl stood by Bulma, both looking equally troubled.  Gohan had been explaining the situation to them.

                “Vegita and Goku did a permanent fusion with earrings?” Bulma asked again, feeling faint.

                “Yeah.  His name is Vegitto,” Krillin began and broke off as Vegitto teleported into the room.  Krillin shut his mouth.  Something about Vegitto was very intimidating.  And he’d thought Gojita had been bad enough to be around.

                “The boys are sleeping,” Gohan said as Vegitto looked toward the room where they slept.

                Chi Chi walked right up to Vegitto and boldly reached to touch one of the earrings.

                “Just great. What you two don’t think of to get yourselves into.  And I thought Goten and Trunks gave me headaches…”

                “Are you both of them together?” Bulma asked, staring as if she could separate them by sheer concentration. 

                “I am Vegitto.  I am neither Vegita nor Kakkarotto.”
