Tenkaichi Budoukai - What does it mean?

Tenkaichi Budoukai, very roughly, translates into, "The World's Greatest Martial Arts Tournament". Now, I know you are saying, "How did you get that?" Well, it's simple, when you know a little bit about Japanese. Let's take it one step at a time. If you look at it, and you know about Japanese, you see, Te N Ka I Chi Bo Do Ka I. Well…Te and N go together, as do I and Chi, plus, Ka and I, and, Do has an U at the end of that. So, that makes it: Ten Ka Ichi Bu Dou Kai. Ok, Tenkaichi Budoukai, but, what does it mean. Well, let's go through this one step at a time again. Ten, means, Heavens or Sky, while, Ka means Under or Beneath. Ichi means One, First, or Greatest. Bu is for Martial Arts. Dou means, a Way of, and, Kai means Meeting. Your saying, "How did you get, Heavens Beneath One Martial Arts Way of Meeting?" Hey, don't take it seriously; Japanese is very mixed up, comparing it to the English language. Ok, once more, we will go through this, step by step again.

Ten/Heaven or sky with ka/under or beneath makes, "Under the Heavens". So, that isn't in the title, "The World's Greatest Martial Arts Tournament" Well, think, what is under the Heavens? The Earth, or, the World. Ichi, well, that means, one, first, or greatest. Put that with the first part, and you have, "The World's Greatest".

Ba, that means, Martial Arts, so, with Dou, a way of, you have, "Martial Arts". Your saying, "…what happened to the way of?" Ok, well, let's get back into Japanese mood again. When you add those two, with the word, Martial Arts, which is a Japanese way of fighting, which is sort of like a way of already combining it with the first word, which, you just have to tell everyone what you mean anyway.

Ka and Budou, makes, "Martial Art's Meeting". Well, when you get a whole bunch of fighters in a tournament, do they sit and have a conversation? Of course not, they fight, and have the tournament, which, Budoukai means, "Marital Arts Tournament"
